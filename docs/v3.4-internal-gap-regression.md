# v3.4 Internal Gap Regression

This note records the confirmed regression discovered after the `v3.4` inland-water cleanup and visible basemap alignment work.

It exists so the next Codex session can resume from the right seam without rediscovering the same issue.

## Summary

User-reported symptom:

- internal artefacts appear inside map products
- gaps inside the product fill show the basemap beneath

Confirmed conclusion:

- this is a real shipped polygon-hole regression, not just another basemap seam, overlay-order issue, or land/sea mask styling artifact
- there had also been a separate visible basemap alignment issue where the Sea mask sat roughly `1-3px` too far inward from the land border
- user screenshot review and Playwright inspection showed a more severe visible Sea-patch artefact in some places:
  - the Sea mask was not fully contiguous with the surrounding sea
  - the local replacement geometry could produce spike / wedge-like shapes rather than a clean complementary edge
- a later corrective pass targeted those visible-basemap causes directly by simplifying the local patch geometry and disabling the hidden basemap coastline-border stroke

## What This Means

- the visible gap is not only a basemap-conformance problem
- at least some of the visible gap is caused by real holes inside the shipped simplified board polygons
- there may still be a small visible land/sea edge issue at the outer boundary, but the previous severe Sea-patch continuity / spike artefact and the hidden pale coastline stroke have both been directly targeted
- the rendered symptom is broader than the single confirmed `Highland` data-hole example
- `v3.4` therefore remains a recoverable working baseline, but not a clean final geometry baseline
